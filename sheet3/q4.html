Fan

fan circulates air to create a cooling effect by evaporating sweat on the skin.

  • Cooling Mechanism: A fan circulates air to create a cooling sensation but does not lower the actual room temperature.
  • Temperature Control: Fans don't regulate or cool the room's temperature; they just make it feel cooler by increasing airflow.
  • Energy Usage: Fans use significantly less energy compared to air conditioners, making them more cost-efficient.

Air Conditioning

Air conditioning cools the air by removing heat and controlling humidity.

  1. Cooling Mechanism: Fans move air around, while AC systems actively cool and dehumidify the air.
  2. Temperature Control: Fans do not lower room temperature, but AC systems can maintain a specific temperature.
  3. Energy Usage: Fans typically consume less energy than air conditioners, making them more energy-efficient.
